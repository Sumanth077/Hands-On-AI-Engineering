# Wound Care and Management Protocol

## Initial Assessment
- Time since injury (wounds > 6 hours old have higher infection risk).
- Mechanism: clean cut, crush injury, bite, puncture, contaminated.
- Location: proximity to joints, tendons, nerves, vessels, face.
- Tetanus immunisation status.
- Signs of active bleeding or vascular injury (pulsatile bleeding, expanding haematoma, distal pallor/coldness).

## Control of Bleeding
1. Apply direct firm pressure with a clean cloth or gauze for 10-15 minutes continuously.
2. Do NOT remove dressing to inspect — add more layers if blood soaks through.
3. Elevate the limb above heart level.
4. If pressure fails and limb wound: apply tourniquet 5-7 cm above wound, note time applied.
5. Do NOT use tourniquets on neck, groin, or axilla.
6. For deep scalp wounds: apply pressure and suture or staple promptly — scalp bleeds heavily.

## Wound Irrigation (reduces infection risk significantly)
- Irrigate all open wounds copiously with clean running water or 0.9% saline.
- Volume: at least 100-200 ml per centimetre of wound length using a syringe and 18-gauge cannula for high pressure.
- Remove visible debris with gloved hand or forceps.
- Do NOT irrigate puncture wounds (drives contamination deeper).
- Antiseptic solutions (iodine, hydrogen peroxide, alcohol) damage tissue — use saline or clean water only.

## Wound Closure

### Primary Closure (immediate) — suitable for:
- Clean wounds < 6 hours old (< 12 hours on face).
- No signs of infection.
- Adequate wound edges for approximation.

### Suturing technique
- Use interrupted sutures with non-absorbable monofilament (2-0 or 3-0 nylon) for skin.
- Approximate edges without tension; evert wound edges slightly.
- Removal: face 3-5 days; scalp 7 days; extremities 10-14 days; over joints 14 days.

### Delayed Primary Closure (48-72 hours) — use for:
- Wounds > 6 hours old (> 12 hours on face).
- Bite wounds (human or animal).
- Heavily contaminated or crush injuries.
- Pack wound open with moist saline gauze; reassess in 48 hours.

### Do NOT close:
- Puncture wounds.
- Infected wounds (erythema, pus, warmth, swelling).
- Animal or human bites on hands or feet.

## Wound Dressing
- Apply non-adherent dressing covered with absorbent gauze and secure with bandage.
- Change dressing daily or when saturated.
- Instruct patient to keep wound dry for 24-48 hours after closure.

## Tetanus Prophylaxis

| Immunisation status | Clean wound | Dirty/tetanus-prone wound |
|---|---|---|
| Fully immunised (3+ doses, booster < 5 years) | Nothing | Nothing |
| Fully immunised, last booster 5-10 years ago | Nothing | Booster (0.5 ml Td) |
| Fully immunised, last booster > 10 years ago | Booster | Booster |
| Incompletely immunised or unknown | Full course + TIG | Full course + TIG |

TIG = Tetanus Immunoglobulin 250 IU IM (give in opposite limb to vaccine).

## Antibiotic Prophylaxis
- Routine antibiotics are NOT indicated for clean simple wounds.
- Give prophylactic antibiotics for:
  - All bite wounds (human or animal): Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125 mg twice daily for 5 days.
  - Contaminated wounds with signs of infection: same regimen.
  - Penicillin allergy: Doxycycline 100 mg twice daily (not in children < 8 years or pregnancy).

## Signs of Wound Infection (review at 48-72 hours)
- Increasing pain, swelling, warmth, erythema spreading beyond wound.
- Purulent discharge.
- Fever > 38°C.
- Red streaking (lymphangitis).
- Action: open wound if closed, irrigate, prescribe antibiotics, refer if systemic features.

## Referral Criteria
- Wounds involving tendons, joints, nerves, or vessels.
- Compartment syndrome (tense swelling, severe pain on passive stretch, paraesthesia).
- Facial wounds requiring specialist closure.
- Deep contaminated wounds with systemic infection.
- Wounds in immunocompromised patients (diabetes, HIV) that fail to improve.
