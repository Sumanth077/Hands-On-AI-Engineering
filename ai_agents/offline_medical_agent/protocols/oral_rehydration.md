# Oral Rehydration Therapy (ORT) Protocol

## Indications
ORT is first-line treatment for mild to moderate dehydration caused by diarrhoea or vomiting in all age groups. Do NOT use in severe dehydration, altered consciousness, or ileus.

## Assessing Dehydration Severity

### No Dehydration
- Alert, drinking normally.
- Normal skin turgor, moist mouth, normal eyes.
- Normal urine output.
- Action: Home treatment with ORS and continued feeding.

### Mild to Moderate Dehydration (2 or more of the following)
- Restless or irritable.
- Sunken eyes.
- Drinks eagerly, thirsty.
- Skin pinch returns slowly (1-2 seconds).
- Action: ORT in facility as per Plan B below.

### Severe Dehydration (2 or more of the following)
- Lethargic, unconscious, or very weak.
- Sunken eyes.
- Unable to drink or drinks poorly.
- Skin pinch returns very slowly (> 2 seconds).
- Action: IV rehydration (Plan C) — see below.

## Plan A — Home Treatment (No Dehydration)
- Give extra ORS after each loose stool:
  - Children < 2 years: 50-100 ml after each stool.
  - Children 2-10 years: 100-200 ml after each stool.
  - Adults: as much as tolerated (250-500 ml after each stool).
- Continue breastfeeding and normal feeding throughout illness.
- Return immediately if: frequent watery stools, repeated vomiting, high fever, or blood in stool.

## Plan B — ORT in Facility (Mild to Moderate Dehydration)
- Give ORS 75 ml/kg over 4 hours.
- Example: 10 kg child = 750 ml ORS over 4 hours.
- Give small sips frequently (use a spoon for infants).
- Reassess every hour.
- If vomiting, wait 10 minutes then resume more slowly (5 ml every 1-2 minutes).
- After 4 hours reassess: if improved, transition to Plan A; if worsening, switch to Plan C.

## ORS Preparation (WHO Standard Formula)
Per 1 litre of clean water:
- Sodium chloride (table salt): 3.5 g (approximately 1 level teaspoon).
- Trisodium citrate: 2.9 g (or sodium bicarbonate 2.5 g).
- Potassium chloride: 1.5 g.
- Glucose: 20 g (or sucrose 40 g, approximately 8 teaspoons).
- Use pre-packaged WHO ORS sachets when available; mix one sachet in 1 litre of clean water.
- Do NOT add extra sugar or salt.
- Use within 24 hours; discard remainder.

## Plan C — IV Rehydration (Severe Dehydration)
- Establish IV access immediately.
- Adults and children >= 5 years: Ringers Lactate (or 0.9% NaCl) 100 ml/kg over 3 hours.
- Children < 5 years: 100 ml/kg over 3 hours (30 ml/kg fast in first 30 minutes, then 70 ml/kg over 2.5 hours).
- Reassess every 30 minutes; repeat bolus if radial pulse remains weak.
- Start ORS (5 ml/kg/hour) as soon as patient can drink.
- Transfer to higher facility if IV access cannot be established or no improvement.

## Zinc Supplementation (for children 2 months to 5 years with diarrhoea)
- 20 mg/day oral zinc for 10-14 days.
- Infants 2-6 months: 10 mg/day.
- Reduces duration and severity of diarrhoea and prevents recurrence for 2-3 months.

## Feeding During Illness
- Do NOT withhold food. Continue age-appropriate feeds throughout.
- Breastfeeding must continue without interruption.
- Avoid high-sugar drinks (sodas, fruit juice) as they worsen diarrhoea.
