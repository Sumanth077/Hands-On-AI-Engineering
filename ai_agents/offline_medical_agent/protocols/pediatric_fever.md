# Pediatric Fever Management Protocol

## Definition
Fever is defined as a body temperature >= 38.0°C (100.4°F) measured rectally, or >= 37.5°C (99.5°F) axillary in children under 5 years.

## Assessment
- Measure temperature using a calibrated thermometer.
- Assess hydration status: check skin turgor, mucous membranes, urine output, and capillary refill time.
- Check for danger signs: convulsions, altered consciousness, inability to drink, persistent vomiting, severe respiratory distress, stiff neck, bulging fontanelle (infants), petechial or purpuric rash.

## Antipyretic Treatment

### Paracetamol (first-line)
- Dose: 15 mg/kg per dose, every 4-6 hours as needed.
- Maximum: 4 doses in 24 hours (do not exceed 60 mg/kg/day).
- Route: oral or rectal suppository.
- Available preparations: 120 mg/5 ml syrup, 250 mg suppository.

### Ibuprofen (alternative, children >= 3 months and >= 5 kg)
- Dose: 5-10 mg/kg per dose, every 6-8 hours.
- Maximum: 3 doses in 24 hours.
- Route: oral only.
- Do NOT use in children with dehydration, renal impairment, or suspected dengue.

## Supportive Care
- Encourage oral fluids (breastmilk, water, ORS) to prevent dehydration.
- Dress the child in light clothing; avoid over-bundling.
- Do NOT use cold water sponging — it causes shivering and raises core temperature.
- Do NOT use aspirin in children under 16 years (risk of Reye's syndrome).

## Referral Criteria (transfer immediately if any of the following)
- Temperature >= 39.5°C not responding to two doses of antipyretic.
- Age < 3 months with any fever.
- Convulsions — current or within the last 24 hours.
- Signs of severe dehydration.
- Altered mental status or inability to be roused.
- Petechial or purpuric rash (possible meningococcal disease).
- Stiff neck or bulging fontanelle.
- Respiratory rate > 50 breaths/minute (1-12 months) or > 40 breaths/minute (1-5 years).
