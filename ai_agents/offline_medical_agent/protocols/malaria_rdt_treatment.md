# Malaria RDT Testing and Treatment Protocol

## Indications for Testing
Test all patients with fever (>= 37.5°C) or history of fever in the past 48 hours in a malaria-endemic area. Do not treat empirically without a confirmed test result.

## Rapid Diagnostic Test (RDT) Procedure
1. Collect 5 µl finger-prick capillary blood using provided lancet.
2. Apply blood to the sample well using the provided loop or pipette.
3. Add 5 drops of assay buffer to the buffer well.
4. Read result at 15-20 minutes. Do NOT read after 30 minutes.
5. Interpreting results:
   - Negative: Only control line (C) visible.
   - Pf positive (P. falciparum): Control line + Pf test line visible.
   - Pan positive (non-falciparum): Control line + Pan test line visible.
   - Invalid: No control line — repeat with new RDT.

## Treatment by Species

### Uncomplicated P. falciparum malaria (RDT Pf positive)
First-line: Artemether-lumefantrine (AL)
- Weight-based dosing — give twice daily for 3 days:
  - 5-14 kg: 1 tablet (20 mg / 120 mg) per dose.
  - 15-24 kg: 2 tablets per dose.
  - 25-34 kg: 3 tablets per dose.
  - >= 35 kg: 4 tablets per dose.
- Give with food or full-fat milk to ensure absorption.
- If vomiting within 30 minutes of dose, repeat dose.

### Non-falciparum malaria (RDT Pan positive)
First-line (P. vivax or P. ovale): Chloroquine
- Day 1: 10 mg/kg base (max 600 mg).
- Day 2: 10 mg/kg base (max 600 mg).
- Day 3: 5 mg/kg base (max 300 mg).
- Primaquine for radical cure (P. vivax relapse prevention): 0.25 mg/kg/day for 14 days.
  - Do NOT give primaquine without G6PD screening — causes haemolysis in G6PD-deficient patients.
  - Do NOT give primaquine in pregnancy.

### RDT Negative with strong clinical suspicion
- If RDT negative but patient has high fever, chills, headache, and epidemiological exposure:
  - Treat for uncomplicated falciparum malaria while arranging blood film microscopy.
  - Document reason for treating RDT-negative patient.

## Severe Malaria — Recognition and Emergency Management

### Suspect severe malaria if any of:
- Impaired consciousness or coma (Blantyre/GCS < 11).
- Respiratory distress (deep breathing / acidosis).
- Severe anaemia (Hb < 7 g/dl or severe pallor).
- Hypoglycaemia (blood glucose < 2.2 mmol/L or < 40 mg/dL).
- Haemoglobinuria (dark/black urine).
- Repeated convulsions (> 2 in 24 hours).
- Prostration (unable to sit/stand unaided).
- Jaundice with evidence of vital organ dysfunction.

### Immediate actions for severe malaria
1. Establish IV/IO access.
2. Check blood glucose: if < 2.2 mmol/L, give 10% dextrose 5 ml/kg IV (child) or 50 ml of 50% dextrose IV (adult) and recheck.
3. Initiate artesunate IV/IM (first-line for severe malaria):
   - 2.4 mg/kg IV/IM at 0, 12, and 24 hours, then every 24 hours.
   - If IV artesunate not available: artesunate rectal capsule (10 mg/kg) as pre-referral treatment in children < 6 years.
4. Treat convulsions: diazepam 0.3 mg/kg IV slowly (max 10 mg) or rectal (0.5 mg/kg).
5. Transfer to higher facility urgently after initiating treatment.
6. Do NOT give fluid bolus routinely in severe malaria — risk of pulmonary oedema.
   - Give maintenance fluids only unless clear evidence of hypovolaemia.

## Special Populations

### Pregnancy
- First trimester: Quinine + clindamycin for 7 days (avoid artemisinin in 1st trimester if possible).
- Second and third trimester: Artemether-lumefantrine (AL) is safe.
- Do NOT give primaquine in pregnancy.

### Infants < 5 kg
- AL not licensed below 5 kg; use quinine in infants under 5 kg.

## Follow-up
- Review all patients at 48-72 hours.
- If fever persists at 72 hours: obtain blood film microscopy if available and consider treatment failure or alternative diagnosis.
- Repeat RDT at day 3 and day 28 for treatment failure assessment where resources permit.
