# Anaphylaxis Emergency Protocol

## Definition
Anaphylaxis is a severe, life-threatening systemic hypersensitivity reaction. Onset is typically within minutes of exposure to a trigger (medications, insect stings, food, latex).

## Recognition — Suspect Anaphylaxis If:
Sudden onset (minutes to 2 hours) involving skin/mucosa PLUS at least one of:
- Respiratory compromise (wheeze, stridor, dyspnoea, SpO2 < 92%)
- Cardiovascular collapse (hypotension, syncope, tachycardia)

OR two or more of the following after exposure to a likely allergen:
- Urticaria, pruritis, flushing, or angioedema
- Vomiting, abdominal cramps, diarrhoea
- Bronchospasm or upper airway obstruction
- Hypotension or end-organ dysfunction

## Immediate Actions (perform simultaneously)

### 1. Call for help
Alert all available clinical staff immediately.

### 2. Adrenaline (Epinephrine) — FIRST-LINE, give immediately
- Route: Intramuscular (IM) injection into the anterolateral mid-thigh.
- Adult dose: 0.5 mg (0.5 ml of 1:1000 adrenaline).
- Child dose: 0.01 mg/kg IM (max 0.5 mg). Practical guide:
  - < 6 years: 0.15 mg (0.15 ml of 1:1000)
  - 6-12 years: 0.3 mg (0.3 ml of 1:1000)
  - > 12 years: 0.5 mg (0.5 ml of 1:1000)
- Repeat every 5 minutes if no improvement. Most patients require 1-2 doses.

### 3. Position
- Lay patient flat with legs elevated (unless breathing is compromised — then sit up).
- Do NOT allow patient to stand or walk.

### 4. High-flow oxygen
- Apply 10-15 L/min via non-rebreather mask immediately.

### 5. IV access
- Establish large-bore IV access (two lines if possible).
- IV fluid bolus: 500-1000 ml 0.9% sodium chloride (adult); 10-20 ml/kg (child). Repeat if hypotension persists.

## Secondary Treatments (after adrenaline)

### Antihistamine (H1 blocker) — adjunct only, do NOT give instead of adrenaline
- Chlorphenamine: Adult 10 mg IV/IM; Child 0.1 mg/kg IV/IM.
- Relieves urticaria and pruritus but does NOT prevent or treat airway obstruction or shock.

### Hydrocortisone — adjunct to prevent biphasic reaction
- Adult: 200 mg IV/IM.
- Child: 50-100 mg IV/IM (2.5 mg/kg up to 100 mg).
- Onset is delayed (4-6 hours); give after adrenaline.

### Bronchodilator (for persistent bronchospasm)
- Salbutamol nebulised: 2.5-5 mg (adult), 2.5 mg (child < 25 kg) via nebuliser.

## Monitoring
- Continuous: pulse, BP, respiratory rate, SpO2, level of consciousness.
- Monitor for minimum 6 hours after adrenaline (biphasic reactions can occur 1-72 hours later).
- 12-24 hours observation if severe reaction or biphasic history.

## Referral / Transfer
Transfer to a higher-level facility if:
- More than 2 doses of adrenaline required.
- Refractory hypotension or bronchospasm.
- Airway compromise requiring intubation.
- Biphasic reaction.
- All children with anaphylaxis should be transferred after stabilisation.
